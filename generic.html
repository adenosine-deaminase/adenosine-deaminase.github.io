Predicting NASDAQ indexes
during the COVID-19 pandemic

Covid-19, the most significant "black swan incident" in 2020, seemed to add huge uncertainties to the stock market. Between 20 February 2020 and ended on 7 April, the global stock market experienced the fastest fall in financial history and the most devastating crash since the Wall Street Crash of 1929. NASDAQ indexes, one kind of critical stock indexes in the US stock market, were no exception.

Introduction

Background

There are countless factors influencing the stock market. It’s impossible to study them exhaustively. Here we try to study several aspects including Google Trends and covid-19 situation (new cases/deaths).

Google Trends provides a time series index of the volume of queries users enter into Google in a given geographic data. Depending on the time range, the data was normalized to [0,100]. Google trends data are available. Furthermore, the search queries were classified into 25 categories on the top level, 274 on the second level, and 629 on the third level, providing an abundant database for us to analyze what people are concerning during a certain time period.

Previous researches show that the addition of relevant Google Trends predictors to the random-work model for initial claims for unemployment benefits in the US seemed to help in identifying at least two of the turning points in the series and showed reduced mean absolute error (MAE) during the recession though the total MAE was higher.[1] Here we conduct similar analysis on NASDAQ indexes, especially in the year of 2020. We will focus on the influence of Google trends while also looking at two significant numbers that we’ve been concerning for almost one year -- the new Covid-19 cases and deaths.

Research Questions

How does the AR-1 Model perform on predicting NASDAQ indexes?

There are many queries on google whose time series seem to be related with the covid situation. Do they help to improve the performance of the AR-1 model?

Among all the Google Trends categories, which are good predictors of NASDAQ indexes?

(2015-2020) Predicting NASDAQ-healthcare

In this section we study the performance of AR-1 models on predicting the NASDAQ-Healthcare index (^IXHC).

Dataset

At first, we chose two NASDAQ indexes: NASDAQ-Healthcare (^IXHC) and NASDAQ-Biotech (^NBI) based on a naive thought that those indexes might be to more extent related to Covid-19. The time range was set as 2015/12/01~2020/12/01 with resolution of one week. As shown below, These two indexes seemed to be one the same boat, rising and falling together. Hence we only took NASDAQ-Healthcare in the following regression analysis.

Regression Analysis

Baseline model

As Nelson and Plosser (1982) and many subsequent authors have pointed out, it is very common for macroeconomic data to be represented as a random walk. For a random walk, the best univariate forecast for y_{t} is simply y_{t-1}.

Trends Model

The trends model is simply the random walk model with google trends data on the topic vaccine being an additional feature.

It seems that in the long run (5 years in our case), Google Trends on the topic vaccine is likely to make no contribution to the random walk model.

Summary

In summary, in this section we evaluated the performance of the AR-1 model on predicting NASDAQ-Healthcare index in recent 5 years. Results showed that the best univariate predictor is just the one-step-ahead value i.e. random walk model. When playing with additional predictors on the AR-1 model, we found that the NASDAQ-Biotech index slightly improved the random walk model fit while the Google trends on the topic vaccine seemed to have no contribution to the model which is in contrary to our intuition. Our next step is to study the influence of more Google Trends topics and the covid cases/deaths on the AR-1 Model in the year 2020.

(2020) Predicting NASDAQ-composite

In this section we study the performance of AR-1 models on predicting the NASDAQ-Composite index (^IXIC) in 2020. More additional predictors were studied.

Dataset

From now on, we study the NASDAQ-Composite index (^IXIC). The time range was set as 2015/12/01~2020/12/01 with resolution of one day. In order to explore the changes of the stock market as far as possible, we utilize the COVID-19 data in the US and focus on the new cases.

We also came out with 9 Google Trends topics that might influence the stock market in 2020. Both trends in the US and worldwide were obtained to see if there’s any difference since the NASDAQ index characterizes the US market.

Regression Analysis

We did a rolling-window prediction where the training set we determined as the all the data before day t. The startpoint of prediction was set as 2020/02/01 to ensure that there are enough data points to predict the first value.

We can see that the deaths model has ~5% smaller total MAE than the other two models.

We then take a look at the MAE values at 4 turning points in 2020 which were specified in the following figure:

We could see that only the deaths model shows less MAE than the base model during all the four turning points.

We can see that the Google Trends on the topic lockdown among the world has a rather low p-value compared with other predictors. We also compared the total MAE of trends model with that of base model. The improvement ratio was plotted:

We could see that Google Trends on topics such as covid (in the world and the US), vaccine (in the world), and loan (in the US) improved the total MAE. We also did a turning-point MAE analysis on the trends models. The following figure plots the improvement of MAE compared with base model for each trends model:

In the first period, Google Trends topic Italy shows the largest improvement of MAE. That period corresponds to the first peak of Google Trends on the topic Italy, which might be related to the rapidly increasing Covid-19 cases reported in Italy.

In the second period, Italy also shows the largest improvement. Interestingly, the US presidential election also improved the MAE. Referring to the Google Trends data on the election, we could see a small peak in that period.

In the third period, lockdown produced the largest increase of MAE but this is hard to explain because there were no obvious change on the Trends about lockdown in that period.

In the 4th period, it seems that no trends significantly increased the MAE, however, the election produced the largest decrease of MAE, which might indicate that the public concern on election itself is not a good predictor for NASDAQ index because because it might originates from both good news and bad news about the election.

To make it clearer, we plot the improvement of MAE for each trends model at 4 turning points as shown below:

We can see that only queries related with Italy, no matter in the US or the world, show significant improvement of MAE during the first turning point though there exists relatively small improvements of MAE during the 4 periods.

Summary

In summary, in this section we evaluated the performance of the AR-1 model on predicting NASDAQ-Composite index in 2020. Results showed that the addition of deaths due to Covid-19 increased the MAE during the whole period and the 4 turning points compared with the random walk model while the model added with new cases of Covid-19 does not. We also studied the google trends of 9 topics. We found that ‘covid’ improves the total MAE best while ‘Italy’ improves the MAE significantly at the first turning point which corresponds to the onset of Covid-19 pandemic in Italy.

(2020) Predicting NASDAQ-composite

After zooming into specific topics , we’d like to take a step further and expand our research objects to more general topics in order to find which aspects of life are more likely to improve NASDAQ predictions. Before we do any data analysis, we download all levels of google trends topics in 2020 and conduct data wrangling to meet our needs.

First, we again set our model as basic AR-1 model. Each time we add one google trends variable into out-sample regression and make a comparison with our base model by calculating MAE and ratio of improvement.

To our surprise, a large proportion of topics having good performances are about entertainment and leisure in life which may indicate that during the epidemic period, people miss social and outdoor activities and are very eager to find more brand new ways to entertain themselves.Their desire for past collective life causes changes to their behavior which has an indirect impact on stock price.

What’s more, we find from the table below, that the search for vulnerable groups also has a great impact for improving predictions. It makes sense since the old and weak are more likely to get infected by corona and need more attention and care.

In order to find which topics combined together can fit well with our model. We used a Bayesian method known as ‘spike and slab’ regression introduced in Predicting the Present with Google Trends . This method assumes every variable has an extra coefficient according to Bernoulli distribution which shows how suitable the variable should enter a regression. MCMC algorithm is applied to calculate the posterior distribution of all coefficients.

We first use the topics from the first level(the most general one) and do the sampling for 5000 times. News, Reference, Internet & Telecom, Pets & Animals, Health are the five topics which are most likely to enter the regression. We then take subtopics from them and run the algorithm again. Broadcast & Network News and Aging & Geriatrics are most influential topics. Finally, for the bottom level. we find the spike and slab technique assigned high posterior probabilities to political polls & Surveys and Campaigns & Elections which is not surprised because 2020 U.S. election has large impact on stock.

From the analysis above, Leisure, Healthcare, Politics are three main topics in this unprecedented year. In fact, we believe they are not only good indicators of Stock but also a strong sketching of what we are experiencing now.
